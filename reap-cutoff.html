Understanding REAP Cutoff

The **REAP 2026 Cutoff** refers to the minimum rank or percentile required by a candidate to get admission into their desired engineering college and branch in Rajasthan. The cutoff is released after each round of counseling by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG).

Admission is primarily based on **JEE Main Rank**, followed by **Class 12th Percentile** and **Diploma Marks**. Candidates with a higher rank or percentage have a better chance of securing seats in top government colleges like MBM University, CTAE Udaipur, and RTU Kota.

The cutoff varies for different categories (General, SC, ST, OBC, MBC, EWS) and for candidates from Outside Rajasthan (OR).

Factors Determining REAP Cutoff

The cutoff for REAP 2026 is not fixed and changes every year based on several factors:

  • Number of Applicants: Higher number of applicants usually leads to a higher cutoff.
  • JEE Main Difficulty: If the JEE Main exam is difficult, the cutoff rank might drop.
  • Seat Matrix: The total number of seats available in a specific branch and college.
  • Category Reservation: Cutoffs are significantly lower for SC, ST, and OBC-NCL candidates compared to General candidates.
  • Previous Year Trends: Past cutoffs are often a good indicator of future trends.
How to Check REAP 2026 Cutoff?

Candidates can follow these steps to check the opening and closing ranks once the counseling begins:

  1. Visit the official REAP website.
  2. Look for the "Merit List" or "Cutoff" link on the homepage.
  3. Select the Round Number (Round 1, 2, or 3).
  4. Download the PDF file containing the college-wise and branch-wise cutoff.
  5. Search for your desired college to see the minimum rank required.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: What is the cutoff for MBM University Jodhpur?

For the CSE branch, the cutoff usually closes around the 90-95 percentile for General category candidates. For other branches, it may vary between 70-85 percentile.

Q2: How is the REAP 2026 Cutoff decided?

The cutoff is determined based on the number of applicants, difficulty level of JEE Main, Class 12th results, and the total number of available seats in participating colleges.

Q3: Is there a separate cutoff for Outside Rajasthan candidates?

Yes, 15% of seats are reserved for Outside Rajasthan (OR) candidates. Since they are treated as General category, their cutoff is generally higher than state domicile candidates.

Q4: Where can I check the official REAP Cutoff?

The official cutoff and merit list will be released on the REAP official website (reap2026.in or ceg.rajasthan.gov.in) after each round of counseling.

Q5: Does the cutoff vary for different rounds?

Yes, the cutoff usually drops in subsequent rounds (Round 2 and Internal Sliding) as seats remain vacant or candidates withdraw.
