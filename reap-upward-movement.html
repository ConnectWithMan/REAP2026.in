How to Upgrade College in REAP 2026

Complete Guide to Upward Movement & Seat Upgradation

Overview of Upward Movement

The REAP 2026 Upward Movement is a facility provided by CEG Rajasthan for candidates who are not satisfied with their allotted seat in the initial rounds. It allows candidates to upgrade their seat to a higher preference college or branch based on their merit and vacant seats.

To participate in this process, candidates must have reported to their allotted institute and paid the requisite fee. If a candidate is allotted a new seat during upward movement, their previous seat is automatically cancelled.

This process is strictly based on the choices filled by the candidate during the initial registration. No new choices can be added.

Steps to Apply for Upward Movement

Step 1: Physical Reporting

First, you must report to the college allotted to you in the first or second round. Submit your original documents and pay the admission fee. Only reported candidates can apply for upward movement.

Step 2: Submit Consent Online

Log in to the REAP 2026 candidate portal. Look for the "Apply for Upward Movement" option. Select "Yes" to give your consent for seat upgradation.

Step 3: Seat Allotment Processing

The system will check for vacancies in colleges that are higher in your preference list than your current seat. If a seat is available and your merit allows, you will be upgraded.

Step 4: Check Result

On the declared date, check the Upward Movement result. If upgraded, download the new allotment letter.

Step 5: Report to New College

If your college has changed, you must collect your documents from the old college and report to the new one. If only the branch changed within the same college, internal formalities are required.

Eligibility Criteria for Upgradation

Not everyone is eligible for the upgrade process. Ensure you meet the following conditions:

  • You must have been allotted a seat in Round 1 or Round 2.
  • You must have physically reported to the allotted institute.
  • You must have deposited the required fees and documents.
  • You must have submitted the online consent for Upward Movement on the REAP portal before the deadline.
  • Candidates who did not report or surrendered their seat are NOT eligible.
Important Rules to Remember

Before applying, keep these critical rules in mind:

  1. No Going Back: If you are allotted a new seat in Upward Movement, your previous seat is automatically cancelled. You cannot choose to keep the old seat after upgradation.
  2. Same Choices: The upgrade is based on the choice list you submitted initially. You cannot add or reorder colleges now.
  3. Fee Transfer: The fee paid at the first college is generally transferred to the new college. You might have to pay the difference if the new college fee is higher.
  4. Reporting is Must: If upgraded, you must report to the new college. Failure to do so may result in losing both seats.
What if I don't get upgraded?

If you apply for Upward Movement but do not get a higher preference seat due to lack of vacancies or merit:

1. Seat Retention: Your previously allotted seat remains safe. You can continue your studies there.

2. No Extra Action: You do not need to report again if the seat remains unchanged.

3. Internal Sliding: You may still have a chance for internal branch sliding within the same college if applicable.

FAQs on College Upgradation

Q1: What is Upward Movement in REAP?

Upward Movement is a process where candidates can try to get a higher preference college/branch from their filled choices after reporting to the initially allotted institute.

Q2: What happens to my old seat if I get upgraded?

If you are allotted a new seat during Upward Movement, your previous seat is automatically cancelled and allotted to another candidate. You cannot claim it back.

Q3: Do I need to pay the fee again?

Generally, the fee is transferred to the new college. However, you may need to pay the difference amount if the new college's fee is higher, or claim a refund if it is lower.

Q4: Can I change my choices during Upward Movement?

No, you cannot modify or add new choices. The system will only consider the choices you filled during the initial registration that are above your currently allotted seat.

Q5: Is reporting mandatory for Upward Movement?

Yes, you must physically report to the allotted college in the first round and submit your documents/fee to be eligible for the Upward Movement option.
