Freeze vs Float Options in REAP 2026

Understanding Seat Acceptance: Freeze, Float & Slide

Overview of Seat Acceptance

After the seat allotment result is declared in the REAP 2026 counselling process, candidates are required to exercise their willingness. This involves choosing between options like Freeze, Float, or Slide.

Choosing the right option is crucial as it determines whether you finalize your current seat or keep looking for a better college/branch in subsequent rounds.

Candidates must also pay the Seat Acceptance Fee to confirm their choice, failing which the allotted seat may be cancelled.

Steps to Choose Option

Step 1: Check Allotment

Log in to the REAP 2026 portal on the result day to check which college and branch has been allotted to you.

Step 2: Pay Acceptance Fee

If you are allotted a seat, you must pay the Seat Acceptance Fee online. This confirms that you are accepting the seat (either to freeze or float).

Step 3: Submit Willingness

Select one of the options: Freeze (No Upgrade needed), Float (Yes Upgrade needed), or Slide (Internal Upgrade needed).

Step 4: Download Letter

Download the Provisional Seat Allotment Letter. This document is required during physical reporting.

Step 5: Reporting

If you chose 'Freeze', report to the allotted institute physically with documents. If 'Float', wait for the next round result.

Detailed Definitions

Understanding the nuances of each option is vital:

  • Freeze: You are 100% satisfied. You stop participating in further rounds. You must report to the college to confirm admission.
  • Float: You accept the current seat as a backup but hope for a higher preference in the next round. If upgraded, you MUST accept the new seat; the old one is cancelled. If not upgraded, you keep the old seat.
  • Slide: Similar to Float, but restricted to the SAME institute. You are happy with the college but want a better branch (e.g., shifting from IT to CSE in the same college).
  • No Action: If you do not pay the fee or select an option, your allotted seat is cancelled, and you may be exited from the counselling.
Rules for Upgradation

When choosing Float or Slide, keep these rules in mind:

  1. Automatic Cancellation: If you are upgraded in Round 2, your Round 1 seat is automatically cancelled and offered to someone else. You cannot reclaim the Round 1 seat.
  2. Retention: If no upgrade happens, your Round 1 seat remains safe (provided you paid the fees).
  3. One-Time Fee: The Seat Acceptance Fee is usually paid only once. You don't pay again for the upgraded seat in the next round.
  4. Final Freeze: In the final round, all seats are generally auto-frozen (check specific year brochure).
When to choose what?

Here is a simple guide to help you decide:

1. Choose Freeze IF: You got your #1 choice or you are completely happy with the allotted college and branch.

2. Choose Float IF: You like the allotted seat but feel you have a good chance of getting a higher preference college in the next round.

3. Choose Slide IF: You love the college location/campus but want a different branch there.

FAQs on Freeze vs Float

Q1: What is the main difference between Freeze and Float?

Freeze locks your current seat permanently. Float keeps your current seat but checks for a better one in the next round.

Q2: Do I lose my seat if I choose Float?

No, you only lose your current seat if you are upgraded to a new one. If not upgraded, you retain the old seat.

Q3: Can I change from Float to Freeze?

Yes, if you are satisfied with the result after a subsequent round, you can change your willingness to Freeze.

Q4: Is the Seat Acceptance Fee refundable?

If you withdraw within the specified time frame, a portion of the fee is refunded. Check the specific REAP 2026 refund policy.

Q5: What happens if I don't choose any option?

If you don't pay the fee or select an option by the deadline, your allotted seat is cancelled.
