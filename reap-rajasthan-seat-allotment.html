REAP Rajasthan Seat Allotment 2026

Direct Access to Round-Wise Seat Allotment Results, Upward Movement, Cutoffs & College Reporting

Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP) seat allotment allocates B.Tech/B.E. seats across government and private engineering institutions in Rajasthan based on JEE Main & 12th Board merit.

What is REAP Rajasthan Seat Allotment?

REAP (Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process) Seat Allotment is the central single-window counselling process conducted by the Center for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan on behalf of Rajasthan Technical University (RTU), Kota and Bikaner Technical University (BTU).

Through REAP 2026 seat allotment, eligible candidates secure admission into 1st-year B.Tech / B.E. / B.Arch / B.Design programs offered by top government autonomous engineering colleges, university departments, and private institutes across Rajasthan.

📊

Merit-Based Allocation

Seats are allotted dynamically based on JEE Main percentile (Rank 1 priority) followed by Class 12th PCM / Science stream marks (Rank 2 priority).

🏛️

Category & Quotas

Includes 85% seats for Rajasthan Domiciles, 15% Out-of-Rajasthan quota, 5% TFWS seats, and reservations for EWS, OBC, SC, ST, MBC, and Ex-Servicemen.

🔄

Upward Movement

Candidates can accept their allotted seat and participate in upward movement to upgrade to higher preferred choices in subsequent rounds.

Step-by-Step Guide to Check REAP Seat Allotment

Candidates can check their seat allotment status online by following these simple steps:

1

Visit Official Portal

Go to the official REAP counselling portal.

2

Candidate Login

3

View Allotment Status

Navigate to the Seat Allotment Letter tab to view your allotted college, branch, and category.

4

Acceptance & Fee Payment

5

Physical / Online Reporting

Report to the allotted institute with original documents to confirm admission before the deadline.

Options Available After REAP Seat Allotment

Once a seat is allocated, candidates must choose one of the following official options:

Option Name Action & Meaning Next Step Required
1. Accept & Freeze You are completely satisfied with the allotted college & engineering branch. You do not wish to participate in further rounds. Pay the college admission fee, complete document verification, and finalize your admission.
2. Accept & Upward Movement You retain your currently allotted seat as a safety net while attempting to upgrade to higher preferred options in the next round. Report online/physically to current college, pay reporting fee, and submit the Upward Movement consent form.
3. Reject & Exit You do not accept the allotted seat and wish to withdraw from the counselling process. Your seat will be canceled and offered to another candidate in the next round.

Understanding REAP Upward Movement Process

Upward Movement is a key benefit provided in REAP counselling to ensure students get the best possible college and branch according to their merit.

  • Rule 1: Retention Guarantee — If no higher choice is available during upward movement, your current seat remains intact and locked for you.
  • Rule 2: Automatic Cancellation — If a higher choice is successfully allotted in upward movement, your previous seat is automatically canceled and assigned to another merit applicant.
  • Rule 3: Fee Transfer — The token fee and reporting documents already submitted to your initial college will automatically be transferred to the newly allotted college.

Documents Required for Allotment Reporting

Carry the following original documents along with 2 sets of self-attested photocopies when reporting to your allotted college:

Academic & Score Certificates

  • REAP 2026 Provisional Seat Allotment Letter
  • JEE Main 2026 Scorecard / Rank Card
  • Class 12th Marksheet & Passing Certificate
  • Class 10th Certificate (Date of Birth Proof)

Category & Domicile Proofs

  • Rajasthan Domicile Certificate
  • Category Certificate (OBC-NCL / SC / ST / EWS / MBC)
  • Income Certificate (Mandatory for TFWS Applicants)
  • Ex-Servicemen / PwD Certificate (if applicable)

Identity & Misc Documents

  • Aadhaar Card / Government Photo ID
  • Medical Fitness Certificate (Signed by Govt Doctor)
  • Transfer Certificate (TC) & Migration Certificate
  • 6 Passport Size Recent Photographs

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Got questions about REAP seat allotment? Find clear answers below:

How is REAP seat allotment calculated? ▼
REAP seat allotment is calculated based on candidate merit ranks (JEE Main percentile given first priority, followed by 12th Board marks for remaining seats), candidate category/reservation rules, seat availability matrix, and preference order submitted in the choice filling form.
Can candidates from Outside Rajasthan get seats in REAP? ▼
Yes! 15% of total seats in private self-financing colleges and dedicated Out-of-Rajasthan quotas in government institutes are reserved for candidates belonging to non-Rajasthan states based on JEE Main scores.
Is the reporting fee paid during seat allotment refundable? ▼
Yes, if a candidate formally withdraws within the designated REAP refund window, the token fee is refunded after deducting a nominal processing fee as per AICTE/CEG guidelines.
What happens if no seat is allotted in Round 1? ▼
If no seat is allotted in the initial round, your application automatically moves forward to subsequent rounds (Round 2 / Round 3 / Spot Round) with your saved choice list.
