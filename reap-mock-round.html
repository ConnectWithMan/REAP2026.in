What is REAP Mock Round?

The REAP 2026 Mock Round is a trial seat allotment process conducted by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan. It gives candidates a tentative idea of the college and branch they might get based on their currently filled choices and merit rank. The mock allotment is not a final confirmation of a seat but serves as a crucial guide to help students refine their choice filling strategy before the final deadline.

Mock Round Highlights
Authority Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG), Rajasthan

Basis of Allotment Merit Rank and Initially Filled Choices
Is it Final? No, purely indicative
Next Step Modify or Lock Choices
Result Mode Online via Candidate Login
How Does the Mock Round Work?

The mock round is simulated by the system based on real-time data provided by the candidates:

  • Data Processing: The system analyzes the choices filled by candidates up to a specific mock round cutoff date.
  • Simulated Allotment: It matches these choices against the available seat matrix and the candidates' state merit ranks.
  • Indicative Results: Candidates can view their likely allotted seat, allowing them to gauge the competition for their preferred branches and institutions.
How to Check the REAP Mock Allotment Result?

Follow these steps to check your mock seat allotment once published by CEG Rajasthan:

  • Step 1: Visit the official REAP 2026 admission portal.
  • Step 2: Look for the link that says "Mock Seat Allotment Result" on the homepage.
  • Step 3: Log in using your Form Number (Login ID) and Password.
  • Step 4: Click on 'Submit' to view your tentative college and branch allotment.
  • Step 5: Analyze the result and prepare to adjust or lock your choices in the final choice-filling window.

Benefits of the Mock Round

Participating actively in the mock round provides several strategic advantages for engineering aspirants:

Why You Shouldn't Skip It:

  • Reality Check: Helps you understand if your current choices are realistic and achievable based on your rank.
  • Strategy Correction: Gives you a chance to add more choices or reorder them to secure a better seat.
  • Error Detection: Allows you to easily catch any mistakes or omissions made during the initial choice-filling process.
  • Anxiety Reduction: Familiarizes you with the allotment process, reducing stress and confusion for the final rounds.
What After Mock Round Allotment?

Once you have checked your mock allotment result, you should take the following actions before the final deadline:

  • Review and Modify Choices: If you are not satisfied with the mock allotment, rethink your strategy. Add more college options or reorder your existing preferences.
  • Final Choice Locking: Ensure you lock your final choices before the stipulated deadline. If left unlocked, the system usually auto-locks your last saved preferences.
  • Wait for First Round Allotment: The actual and final seat allotment for round 1 will be processed afterward. Keep in mind that the final result may differ from the mock allotment as other candidates will also alter their choices.
  • Document Readiness: Keep your original documents and fee amounts ready for reporting once the actual allotment is published.
